Pathology & Lesion Effects

  • Corticospinal Tract Lesion:
    A lesion of the corticospinal tract would result in loss of motor function entirely below the cross-sectional area. This typically presents as spastic paralysis (Upper Motor Neuron signs).
  • Transverse Myelitis:
    A lesion of the entire cross-section would also result in total paralysis below the cross-section, involving both motor and sensory pathways bilaterally.
  • Anterior Horn Lesion:
    If only the anterior horn is lesioned, only that nerve root loses motor function on that side. Crucially, the corticospinal tract remains undamaged in this example; thus, descending signals bypass the segmental lesion and continue travelling to the lower spinal cord, preserving distal motor function.
